The Vision

Guided by a deep understanding of Tantra yoga and the natural rhythms of the earth, the retreat embodies the principle of Sacred Functionalism: every form is beautiful, and every beauty serves a purpose.

Designed through the mathematical harmony of the Flower of Life, the landscape dissolves the boundary between indoors and outdoors. Here, transformative disciplines—from the stillness of biphasic sleep to the purifying breathwork of Khechari Mudra and 18-hour fasting cycles—are supported by the very architecture that surrounds you.

Explore the Design

Vāstu-Aligned Architecture

Constructed from the earth itself using rammed earth (taipa), local stone, and reclaimed timber. The masterplan is organized around an open, central Brahmasthan to allow the unhindered flow of prana, ensuring absolute energetic equilibrium across the terrain.

The Main House

The anchor of the retreat. Designed with 9 private suites, a sweeping communal kitchen, and a reception area that transitions guests from the outside world into the sanctuary.

The Yoga Dome

A central geodesic marvel engineered for acoustic resonance. This is the heart of the community, serving daily Tantra yoga instruction, sound healing, and meditation.

The Sanctuaries

6 standalone T1 units spread seamlessly into the landscape, providing secluded spaces for deep introspection, alongside a dedicated T3 unit for the resident staff.

The Healing Landscape

Move through the property along the Spiral Path of Practice, a winding meditation trail that links distinct restorative zones.

In the auspicious Northeast, the recovery pond offers a quiet aquatic mirror for contemplation and elemental balance. To the Southeast, the fire circle acts as a natural earthen amphitheater, providing a space for evening gatherings, chanting, and honoring the Agni element.

The Open-Source Ashram

We are rejecting the "polished vaporware" approach of traditional real estate. This sanctuary is being engineered transparently, entirely on Pop!_OS Linux.

By leveraging FreeCAD for wireframes, Blender for rendering, and Python to procedurally generate the sacred geometry of our structures, we are open-sourcing the architecture of a sanctuary. Follow the raw journey from code execution to pouring the foundations in Portimão.
